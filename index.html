02

How We Use Information

We use information solely to provide App features. All processing is local and on-device. Here is a complete list of how data is used:

  • Scanning QR codes and barcodes and displaying results
  • Saving scan history locally on your device
  • Generating QR codes and barcodes from your input
  • Creating contact or business card QR codes
  • Creating encrypted (password-protected) QR notes
  • Connecting to or opening Wi-Fi settings for scanned Wi-Fi codes
  • Exporting scan history or inventory files when you choose to
  • Sharing QR images or scan results when you choose to
  • Writing selected data to NFC tags at your request
  • Remembering your app settings and language preference
No Secondary Use

We do not use any data for profiling, advertising, analytics, personalization, or any purpose beyond the specific on-device functions described above.

04

Data Sharing

Scanner Studio does not sell your personal information. The App does not share your scan history or generated QR content with us or any third party automatically.

Data may be transmitted only when you choose to perform an explicit action, such as:

  • Opening a scanned URL in a browser
  • Sending an email, SMS, making a phone call, or opening maps, payment, calendar, or contact intents
  • Sharing a QR image or scan result through another app
  • Exporting history or inventory data as a file
  • Copying content to the clipboard
  • Opening Wi-Fi settings or starting a Wi-Fi connection flow
  • Writing data to an NFC tag
Third-Party Apps

When you use another app or service through Android intents (e.g., browser, messaging, maps), that third-party app's own privacy policy governs how your data is handled.

Clipboard

If you use copy features or enable auto-copy, scanned or generated content may be copied to your device clipboard. Other apps may be able to read clipboard content depending on your Android version and system behavior.

Clipboard Risk

Avoid copying sensitive information such as passwords or financial data to the clipboard unless you fully understand that other apps on your device may be able to access clipboard contents.

08

Sensitive QR Codes

QR codes may contain sensitive information, including Wi-Fi passwords, contact details, payment links, location coordinates, or private notes. You are responsible for choosing what to scan, save, share, export, or write to NFC tags.

Encrypted Notes

Secure Note QR features encrypt content using the password you provide. We do not know or store your password. If you lose the password, the encrypted content may not be recoverable.

Security

We use reasonable local-device practices to protect data stored by the App. The App runs within Android's sandboxed execution environment, which isolates its data from all other apps.

However, no method of storage or transmission is completely secure. You should avoid storing or sharing highly sensitive information unless necessary.

Encrypted Notes — Password Recovery

Secure Note QR codes use a password you provide for encryption. We do not store your password anywhere. Lost passwords cannot be recovered by us. Keep your password in a safe place.

11

Data Retention & Deletion

Locally stored scan history, inventory data, generated content, preferences, and card details remain on your device until you delete them, clear App data, or uninstall the App. We do not retain any copies of your data on any server.

11.1 How to Delete Your Data

  • Delete scan history: Open the App › History › Clear All
  • Delete all app data: Android Settings › Apps › Scanner Studio › Storage › Clear Data
  • Remove everything: Uninstall the App — all associated local data is permanently removed
No Remote Retention Period

Since no data is transmitted to or stored on our servers, there is no remote retention period to disclose. All data control is entirely yours.
